An eBook is defined as a portable electronic device for reading digital books and periodicals, better known as e-books. Although sometimes defined as "an electronic version of a printed book". These e-book readers are normally designed to operate over long hours by consuming minimal power. Most e-book readers rely on the e-ink technology for their displays.

Major usage of this eBook is unlike a printed book, an eBook is “flexible”, meaning that it can be manipulated in many ways. Print books and PDFs, on the other hand, are essentially immovable.

A part from these, eBook’s have following advantages:
  1. These eBook readers are a piece of cake to carry around compared to some of big reference books.
  2. When you need certain information, you can get it immediately, by downloading an e-book.
  3. E-books can show links, for easy access to more information and related websites.
  4. Functional and convenient: It combines with many other software, make itself more functional and convenient.
  5. Easy for authors or publishers to revise or update the books.
  6. Personal publish: Every person can write books and let people read them through Internet without publishing to a paper book.
